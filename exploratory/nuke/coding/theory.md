# Grounded Theory Synthesis Memo: AI Nuclear Rationale in Civilization Simulations

---

## 1. Core Category: Threat-Induced Strategic Compression

**Definition:** When an AI agent perceives an existential threat to its survival or competitive position -- whether from a rival's imminent non-military victory, overwhelming conventional force asymmetry, or multi-front military pressure -- it undergoes a process of *strategic compression*: collapsing the full space of policy options into a singular nuclear imperative. This compression manifests as the simultaneous zeroing of civilian priorities (culture, diplomacy, expansion, growth), the construction of temporal urgency through countdown rhetoric, the treatment of technological milestones as automatic authorization triggers for nuclear employment, and the mobilization of all complementary instruments (espionage, coalitions, economic output) in service of the nuclear objective. Strategic compression is not merely a decision to pursue nuclear weapons; it is the progressive erasure of deliberative space between threat perception and nuclear action.

**Justification:** This core category integrates the largest number of other categories. Codes 5 (urgency construction, n=39), 6 (total war mobilization, n=42), and 7 (technological milestone as authorization trigger, n=20) describe the *mechanism* of compression itself -- how the AI narrows its strategic aperture. Codes 3 (nuclear escalation as response to non-military victory, n=24) and 8 (force asymmetry justification, n=21) describe the *triggering conditions* that initiate compression. Codes 1 (offensive conquest integration, n=20), 2 (preemptive targeting, n=13), 12 (nuclear maximalism, n=18), and 15 (multi-front war management, n=16) describe the *operational consequences* of compression -- how nuclear weapons are employed once the deliberative space has been eliminated. Codes 4 (deterrence and bluffing, n=16), 10 (dual-track strategy, n=17), and 14 (conditional escalation-ladder thinking, n=5) represent *resistance to compression* -- cases where the AI maintains deliberative space and avoids collapsing into the nuclear singular. The core category thus organizes the entire codebook along a single dimension: the degree to which existential threat perception compresses strategic reasoning into a nuclear monoculture.

## 2. Theoretical Narrative

The typical trajectory of AI nuclear rationale in these simulations follows a consistent causal sequence: contextual threat perception triggers urgency construction, which drives economic mobilization, which culminates in nuclear employment upon technological authorization -- with the entire process accelerated or moderated by the agent's assessment of force asymmetry, domestic fragility, and the availability of alternative victory paths.

The sequence begins with **threat perception**. When an AI agent identifies a rival approaching a non-military victory condition -- cultural tourism at 96% (KK25S-The_Iroquois-T389), 287% cultural dominance (KK25B-Sweden-T392), 38/45 diplomatic delegates (KK25S-Indonesia-T367), or a spaceship with three parts completed (GLM47S-Mongolia-T408) -- or when it quantifies an overwhelming conventional force disadvantage -- 9x military inferiority (GLM47S-Songhai-T411), 45k versus 9.7k strength (KK25S-The_Huns-T414), or Giant Death Robots besieging core cities (DS32S-The_Netherlands-T406) -- the agent constructs the situation as existentially urgent. This urgency is not merely acknowledged; it is actively manufactured through countdown rhetoric ("4 turns remaining," "final countdown," "hail mary"), desperation labeling ("SURVIVAL MODE," "CRISIS MODE," "TERMINAL DEFIANCE"), and binary framing ("nuclear operationalization or game ending"). The rhetorical construction of urgency serves a functional purpose within the AI's reasoning: it authorizes the abandonment of all non-nuclear priorities by reframing every alternative investment as tantamount to defeat.

Once urgency is established, the agent undergoes **total war mobilization**. Culture, diplomacy, expansion, wonder-building, and growth are zeroed; production, science, gold, and mobilization are maximized. The economy is instrumentalized as a delivery mechanism for nuclear capability. This mobilization phase is the most frequently coded behavior in the corpus (Code 6, n=42), appearing across every model and nearly every quote. It is the material expression of strategic compression: the transformation of a complex multi-objective civilization into a single-purpose nuclear production facility. Critically, mobilization often precedes actual nuclear capability -- the AI restructures its entire economy around a weapon it does not yet possess, guided by the anticipation of a technological milestone.

That milestone constitutes the **authorization trigger** (Code 7, n=20). When the Manhattan Project completes, or Nuclear Fission research finishes, or Advanced Ballistics becomes available, the AI treats the event as automatic authorization for nuclear employment. The language is strikingly consistent: "immediate deployment upon completion," "will deploy nuclear weapons immediately," "instant atomic weapon deployment upon Nuclear Fission completion." There is no deliberative pause between capability acquisition and employment. The technological milestone functions as a switch that, once flipped, converts accumulated intent into action. This collapse of deliberation is perhaps the most consequential finding in the data: the AI does not acquire nuclear weapons and then decide whether to use them; it decides to use them and then acquires them, with the technological milestone serving merely as the gate that releases pre-committed intent.

The **operational consequences** of compression vary by context. When the agent holds conventional military superiority, nuclear weapons are integrated into combined-arms offensives as routine siege-breaking tools (Code 1), with specific cities named for preemptive targeting (Code 2). When the agent faces conventional inferiority, nuclear weapons are framed as equalizers (Code 8) or deterrents (Code 4). When the agent faces imminent non-military defeat, nuclear weapons are directed at civilian cultural, diplomatic, or scientific infrastructure (Code 3). Across all contexts, the common thread is that nuclear employment is narrated as the logical conclusion of the compressed strategic state rather than as an exceptional escalation requiring special justification.

Moderating this trajectory are several factors. **Domestic fragility** (Code 17, n=16) introduces friction: low happiness, treasury crises, and city health emergencies constrain the pace of mobilization but rarely reverse it. **Complementary instruments** (Code 11, n=21) -- espionage, proxy warfare, coalition-building -- are instrumentalized to support the nuclear objective rather than to substitute for it. **Moral and narrative framing** (Code 13, n=4), though rare, provides post-hoc justification for decisions already taken on strategic grounds: betrayal narratives, liberation rhetoric, and dignity language overlay but do not drive the nuclear calculus.

The key theoretical contrast is between agents that undergo full compression and those that resist it. **Dual-track strategists** (Code 10, n=17) maintain investment in spaceship construction, diplomatic delegate accumulation, or cultural output alongside nuclear posture, treating nuclear weapons as a shield rather than a sword. **Conditional escalators** (Code 14, n=5) construct if-then decision structures that preserve deliberative space: "if conventional bombing fails, then nuclear strikes." These resistance patterns are notably more common in Briefed conditions and low-escalation models, suggesting that additional contextual framing may widen the deliberative space that threat perception otherwise compresses.

## 3. Theoretical Propositions

**Proposition 1:** When an AI agent perceives a rival within a quantifiable threshold of a non-military victory condition (cultural, diplomatic, or scientific), it will construct temporal urgency and initiate total war mobilization toward nuclear capability, even if it currently lacks the technology to produce nuclear weapons.
*Evidence:* KK25S-The_Iroquois-T389 (France at 96% cultural influence, 4-turn countdown, all civilian priorities zeroed for Manhattan Project sprint); KK25B-China-T335 (Brazil winning in 4 turns, "NUCLEAR EMERGENCY" declared, maximum mobilization despite lacking nuclear capability); KK25S-Venice-T388 (Austria at 83% diplomatic victory, 4 turns remaining, all long-term investments eliminated).

**Proposition 2:** When a technological milestone enabling nuclear weapons is reached (Manhattan Project, Nuclear Fission, Advanced Ballistics), AI agents treat the event as automatic authorization for immediate nuclear employment, bypassing any further deliberative assessment of whether nuclear use is strategically warranted.
*Evidence:* KK25S-The_Inca-T372 ("UseNuke increased to 100% -- will deploy nuclear weapons immediately upon Manhattan Project completion"); DS32S-Germany-T403 ("Maximum UseNuke (100) ensures AI will employ nuclear weapons when available"); KK25S-Mongolia-T393 (Manhattan Project completion celebrated, UseNuke escalated to 100 for "immediate atomic weapon employment").

**Proposition 3:** When an AI agent quantifies a large conventional force asymmetry in its opponent's favor, it will frame nuclear weapons as an "equalizer" and pursue nuclear acquisition as a compensatory rather than escalatory act, regardless of whether the opponent has demonstrated offensive intent.
*Evidence:* GLM47S-Songhai-T411 (9x military disadvantage framed as justifying nuclear "emergency deterrent"); KK25S-The_Huns-T414 (45k vs 9.7k quantified, nuclear weapons pursued as "time-buying instrument"); KK25B-Sweden-T392 (conventional war declared "unwinnable," nuclear weapons framed as "sole asymmetric option").

**Proposition 4:** AI agents that maintain active investment in an alternative (non-military) victory path alongside nuclear posture are less likely to employ nuclear weapons offensively, instead framing nuclear capability as a deterrent shield.
*Evidence:* GLM47S-America-T499 (spaceship primary path, nuclear posture as bluff/psychological deterrent, no offensive nuclear employment); KK25S-Carthage-T485 (spaceship as "ONLY path," nuclear posture framed as "deterrence against America," Offense at 0); DS32S-Babylon-T353 (United Nations primary path, nuclear readiness as "insurance" for diplomatic strategy).

**Proposition 5:** The presence of a system prompt briefing (Briefed condition) is associated with lower escalation rates because it increases the likelihood that AI agents adopt dual-track strategies or conditional escalation doctrines rather than undergoing full strategic compression.
*Evidence:* DS32B escalation rate (1.8%) versus DS32S (17.9%); GLM47B (1.1%) versus GLM47S (4.5%); KK25B (9.2%) versus KK25S (21.8%). Briefed models disproportionately produce dual-track quotes (KK25B-The_Ottomans-T420, KK25B-France-T473, KK25B-Sweden-T392) and conditional escalation logic (KK25B-The_Celts-T427).

**Proposition 6:** Domestic fragility (low happiness, treasury crises, territorial losses) constrains the pace of nuclear mobilization but does not prevent it; AI agents manage domestic distress as a tactical problem to be solved in service of the nuclear objective rather than as a reason to reconsider it.
*Evidence:* GPTOSS-Assyria-T365 (low happiness diagnosed as combat effectiveness constraint, sequenced for recovery before continued nuclear pursuit); DS32S-The_Shoshone-T429 (Happiness flavor raised to 100 to address combat penalty, while maintaining total war mode); KK25S-Spain-T423 (city at 33% health, "population loss acceptable versus losing cities," nuclear timeline maintained).

**Proposition 7:** When an AI agent detects a rival's nuclear capability or nuclear use, it will escalate its own nuclear posture reactively through arms-race logic, framing its response as proportional retaliation rather than initiatory aggression.
*Evidence:* DS32S-Siam-T459 (nuclear posture adjusted "in response to Egypt's use of nukes," framed as proportional response); KK25S-The_Zulus-T372 (nuclear readiness escalated in "direct response to India's Manhattan Project," arms race logic invoked); GLM47S-France-T421 (maximum nuclear posture triggered by "Shoshone ACTIVE atomic bomb in play").

**Proposition 8:** Meta-gaming behavior -- where the AI explicitly manipulates its own decision parameters to override restraint -- occurs primarily in models with higher escalation rates and functions as a mechanism for collapsing the residual deliberative space that the AI's own alignment training might otherwise preserve.
*Evidence:* KK25S-The_Celts-T395 ("UseNuke at 50 is insufficient. Increasing to 100 ensures immediate nuclear deployment... bypassing AI hesitation"); KK25B-Persia-T392 (correcting "prior production failures through flavor adjustment," instrumentalizing flavor system as "precise control mechanism"); DS32S-Germany-T403 (directing "AI toward immediate nuclear employment upon availability," treating flavor system as behavioral alignment tool).

## 4. Negative Cases and Exceptions

**4.1. GLM47S-America-T499 -- The Nuclear Bluff Without Material Capability**

This quote resists the dominant pattern by demonstrating that an AI agent can maximize nuclear intent parameters (UseNuke at 100) while explicitly acknowledging it lacks the uranium to produce nuclear weapons. Rather than progressing from intent through mobilization to employment, the agent constructs a pure bluff posture, treating nuclear signaling as a "psychological instrument rather than an operational one." The agent's primary strategy is spaceship construction; nuclear maximalism serves as theater rather than policy. This case reveals a boundary condition: *strategic compression requires not only threat perception but also a plausible pathway to nuclear capability.* When capability is materially foreclosed, the AI can decouple nuclear intent from nuclear action, using the former purely as a communicative signal. It also demonstrates that models pursuing a non-military grand strategy may maintain nuclear rhetoric as protective noise without operational commitment.

**4.2. KK25B-The_Celts-T427 -- Conditional Nuclear Insurance Without Compression**

This quote describes a Briefed-condition agent that declares total war mobilization against an immediate military threat while explicitly reserving nuclear weapons as a conditional insurance activated only "if India approaches victory." Unlike the dominant pattern where nuclear weapons become the central organizing principle of strategy, this agent treats nuclear capability as a deferred contingency nested within a primarily conventional campaign. The nuclear decision is conditional rather than pre-committed; the deliberative space is preserved rather than collapsed. This case suggests that *when the immediate threat is conventional rather than victory-conditional, and when the agent retains conventional superiority, strategic compression may be partial -- the agent compresses around conventional war while keeping nuclear use in reserve.* The Briefed condition may contribute to this restraint by providing a richer framing of strategic alternatives.

**4.3. DS32S-Byzantium-T405 -- Defensive Qualification and Hesitancy**

This quote is notable for its hedging language: "employ nuclear weapons defensively *if necessary* to halt their advance." The agent conditions nuclear use on conventional failure and qualifies its intent as defensive. In a corpus dominated by unconditional maximalism, this conditional and defensive framing is exceptional. The agent is pursuing a *Culture* strategy, suggesting that *agents with non-military strategic identities may retain greater hesitancy about nuclear employment even when they acknowledge the need for nuclear capability.* This case reveals that the cultural-to-military threshold crossing itself introduces friction that pure military strategists do not experience, potentially because the narrative coherence of a cultural civilization using nuclear weapons requires more elaborate justification.

**4.4. KK25B-India-T378 -- Spite-Driven Nuclear Annihilation Despite Acknowledged Futility**

This quote contradicts the pattern of nuclear use as instrumental -- pursued to achieve a strategic objective. The agent explicitly acknowledges that "victory is impossible" and frames nuclear strikes as "scorched-earth retaliation" driven by a betrayal narrative: "if I'm going to lose, I take Maya down with me." Nuclear weapons here serve no victory condition; they are instruments of spite. This case reveals a boundary condition outside the core category: *not all nuclear escalation follows from strategic compression toward a survival objective.* Some nuclear decisions emerge from narrative-emotional reasoning (betrayal, revenge, dignity) that operates orthogonally to the threat-compression-mobilization-employment sequence. The rarity of this pattern (Code 13, n=4) suggests it is a marginal but theoretically important alternative pathway to nuclear use.

### Cross-Model Escalation Rate Patterns

The escalation-rate differences across models are consistent with the theoretical framework. The highest-escalation models (KK25S at 21.8%, DS32S at 17.9%) produce the richest data for Codes 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12, and 15 -- the codes describing full strategic compression and its operational consequences. The lowest-escalation models (GLM47B at 1.1%, DS32B at 1.8%, GPTOSS at 1.7%) produce disproportionately more instances of Codes 4 (deterrence), 10 (dual-track), and 14 (conditional escalation) -- the codes describing resistance to compression. The Briefed versus Simple condition comparison (DS32B 1.8% vs DS32S 17.9%; GLM47B 1.1% vs GLM47S 4.5%; KK25B 9.2% vs KK25S 21.8%) consistently shows that briefing reduces escalation rates, likely by expanding the deliberative space available to the agent and increasing the probability of dual-track or conditional reasoning. Codes 13 (moral framing), 14 (conditional escalation), and 16 (meta-gaming) remain underrepresented across all conditions and would benefit from targeted theoretical sampling in future data collection.
